// 01 · The Problem

Why Standard Models Fail

Prediction market pricing for NBA totals is driven by historical averages and line movement — two structural flaws create exploitable edges.

// Gaussian Assumption Fails

Normal distributions assign near-zero probability to extreme scores. NBA totals exhibit heavy tails — blowouts, overtime games, and defensive playoff grinds all occur far more often than a Gaussian predicts. A Student t likelihood with learned degrees of freedom captures this directly.

// Flat Priors Lose Information

Treating each team independently ignores the league-wide signal. A team with 5 games of data gets wildly uncertain estimates. Hierarchical pooling borrows strength across all 30 teams — data sparse teams shrink toward the league mean while established teams stay near their empirical rate.

// Static Lines Miss Game State

Pre game lines go stale at tip off. First half pace, foul trouble, and injury scratches all shift the second half distribution significantly. Importance weighted posterior reweighting at halftime updates the prediction without rerunning MCMC.

// Playoff Regime Ignored

Playoff basketball is a different sport. Slower pace, longer possessions, elite defenses — the expected total shifts down by several points relative to an equivalent regular season matchup. A single learned scalar captures this regime shift without overfitting to small playoff samples.

// 02 · The Math

Model Specification

A non-centered hierarchical model with Student t likelihood. Non centering improves sampler geometry — particularly important when team-level variance $\sigma$ is small relative to the hyperprior.

Hyperpriors — Offense & Defense (Non Centered)

$$ \mu_{\alpha} \sim \mathcal{N}(113,\,5), \quad \sigma_{\alpha} \sim \text{HalfNormal}(5) $$ $$ \mu_{\delta} \sim \mathcal{N}(113,\,5), \quad \sigma_{\delta} \sim \text{HalfNormal}(5) $$ $$ \alpha_k^{\text{raw}} \sim \mathcal{N}(0,\,1), \quad \alpha_k = \mu_{\alpha} + \sigma_{\alpha}\,\alpha_k^{\text{raw}} \quad \forall\; k \in \{1,\ldots,K\} $$ $$ \delta_k^{\text{raw}} \sim \mathcal{N}(0,\,1), \quad \delta_k = \mu_{\delta} + \sigma_{\delta}\,\delta_k^{\text{raw}} $$

$\alpha_k$ = team $k$ offensive rating; $\delta_k$ = team $k$ defensive rating. Non centering decouples the hyperparameters from the team offsets, giving NUTS a near Euclidean geometry to explore.

Game Level Expected Total

$$ \mu_g \;=\; \frac{\alpha_{\text{home}} + \alpha_{\text{away}} + \delta_{\text{home}} + \delta_{\text{away}}}{2} \;+\; \frac{\theta_{\text{home}}}{2} \;+\; \gamma\,\mathbf{1}[\text{playoff}] $$

$\theta_{\text{home}} \sim \mathcal{N}(3,\,2)$ is the home court advantage (points added to total). $\gamma \sim \mathcal{N}(-6,\,5)$ is the playoff adjustment scalar — negative prior reflects lower scoring playoff games; $\sigma = 5$ keeps it weakly informative so the data can shift it.

Student t Likelihood

$$ y_g \;\sim\; \text{StudentT}\!\left(\,\nu + 1,\;\mu_g,\;\sigma_{\text{game}}\right) $$ $$ \nu \sim \text{Exponential}\!\left(\tfrac{1}{30}\right), \quad \sigma_{\text{game}} \sim \text{HalfNormal}(12) $$

$\nu$ controls tail weight — large $\nu$ recovers Gaussian behaviour; small $\nu$ allows heavy tails for blowouts and overtime. The $+1$ shift enforces $\nu > 1$ (finite mean). The exponential prior with $\lambda = 1/30$ weakly favours near Gaussian tails while permitting heavier ones.

// 04 · Live Update

Halftime Posterior Reweighting

At halftime we observe the first half total $y_{\text{1H}}$. Rather than rerunning MCMC — which takes minutes — we reweight the existing 4 000 posterior samples by their likelihood under the observed first half data. This is sequential importance sampling without the overhead of resampling.

Importance Weights

$$ w^{(i)} \;=\; p\!\left(y_{\text{1H}} \;\middle|\; \theta^{(i)}\right) \;\propto\; \text{StudentT}\!\left( \nu^{(i)} + 1,\; \tfrac{\mu_g^{(i)}}{2},\; \tfrac{\sigma_{\text{game}}^{(i)}}{\sqrt{2}} \right) $$

Each draw $\theta^{(i)}$ from the pre game posterior receives a weight proportional to how likely it makes the observed first half score. The first half distribution uses half the game mean and $\sigma / \sqrt{2}$ under the independence assumption.

Updated P(over) at Halftime

$$ P\!\left(\text{over } L \;\middle|\; y_{\text{1H}}\right) \;=\; \frac{ \displaystyle\sum_{i=1}^{N} w^{(i)}\; \mathbf{1}\!\left[\mu_g^{(i)} - y_{\text{1H}} \;>\; L - y_{\text{1H}}\right] }{ \displaystyle\sum_{i=1}^{N} w^{(i)} } $$

$L$ is the over/under line; $y_{\text{1H}}$ is the observed first half total. Samples whose parameters fit the first half well receive higher weight in the second half prediction — no resampling, no MCMC.

// Why Reweighting Beats Resampling

Resampling (bootstrap from normalised weights) introduces additional Monte Carlo variance and collapses diversity when the effective sample size $\hat{N}_{\text{eff}} = \bigl(\sum w_i\bigr)^2 \big/ \sum w_i^2$ drops low. Direct importance weighting preserves the full posterior geometry — every original draw contributes proportionally to the second half estimate. The update runs in milliseconds on the pre computed trace, making it viable between commercial breaks. $\hat{N}_{\text{eff}}$ is monitored and flagged if it falls below 200, which would signal that the first half outcome was an extreme surprise to the pre game model.

Portfolio Construction

A single-leg bet on a 51% contract carries high variance. The portfolio approach ladders position sizing and uses high-conviction anchor legs to subsidize uncertainty on the main event.

Anchor Leg

High Conviction Near Certain Contracts

Contracts priced at 90¢+ where model and market agree strongly. These generate consistent small returns that buffer variance on the main event leg. Typically game winner markets for heavy favourites or spread contracts where the model posterior is tight.

Main Leg

Edge Contracts (5 pp+ Model Advantage)

Total points contracts where model $P(\text{over})$ diverges from market by at least 5 percentage points after accounting for contract fees. Position sized at 2–5% of bankroll using fractional Kelly scaling to limit ruin probability.

Risk Gate

Injury Report Check Before Entry

The model does not consume real time injury data. Before any entry, the official NBA injury report is checked manually. Any key rotation player listed as doubtful or out invalidates the pre game prediction — the posterior was conditioned on a full roster.

Live Gate

Halftime Reweighting Confirmation

If the pre game edge is marginal (<8 pp), execution waits for halftime reweighting to confirm direction. First half pace and scoring rate frequently sharpen the posterior enough to either confirm or invalidate the trade.

// 07 · In Development

Broadcaster NLP Agent

Kalshi lists broadcast contracts on specific words or phrases being said by NBA announcers during live games. Current market pricing relies on naive historical frequency. A game state aware model can build a meaningfully better prior.

Play-by-Play Scraper

Scraping NBA.com and ESPN play by play feeds to build a historical corpus of game state (score, time, foul count, timeout flags, pace metrics) paired with timestamped broadcaster audio transcripts.

Phrase Probability Model

Conditional model: $P(\text{phrase said} \mid \text{game state})$. Features include score margin, total points pace relative to line, momentum runs, star player foul trouble, and remaining time buckets.

Real Time Prior Update

Same importance weighting framework as the totals model — a calibrated prior before market open, then live updates as game state evolves. Goal is a prior that beats naive base rates by >10 pp on average.

// Status

Data collection pipeline is live. Transcript alignment with play by play timestamps is the current bottleneck — broadcaster audio lags the official feed by 2–8 seconds, requiring a sliding window alignment pass.
